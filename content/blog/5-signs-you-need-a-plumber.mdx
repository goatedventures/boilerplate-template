Most plumbing problems start small. A slow drain here, an odd noise there. But left unchecked, minor issues can turn into major — and expensive — repairs.

Here are five warning signs that mean it's time to call a professional.

## 1. Persistently Slow Drains

If your sink or shower is draining slowly despite trying a plunger or shop-bought drain cleaner, there's likely a deeper blockage in your pipes. Professional drain clearing equipment can sort this quickly without damaging your pipework.

## 2. Low Water Pressure

A sudden drop in water pressure could indicate a leak somewhere in your system, a problem with your stopcock, or a build-up of limescale in older pipes. It's worth getting checked before it gets worse.

## 3. Unusual Noises from Pipes

Banging, gurgling, or whistling from your pipes is never normal. These sounds often point to air in the system, loose fittings, or water hammer — all of which a qualified plumber can diagnose and fix.

## 4. Damp Patches or Water Stains

If you notice damp patches on walls, ceilings, or floors, there's likely a hidden leak. The longer you leave it, the greater the risk of structural damage and mould growth. Early detection saves money.

## 5. Your Boiler Keeps Losing Pressure

If you're constantly topping up your boiler pressure, there may be a leak in the heating system or an issue with the pressure relief valve. A Gas Safe registered engineer can diagnose and repair this safely.
